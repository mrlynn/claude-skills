# Resume Tailoring Strategies

## The Core Principle

**One size fits none.** Every job application needs a tailored resume because:
- ATS systems scan for exact keyword matches
- Hiring managers spend 6 seconds on initial resume review
- Generic resumes get filtered out before humans see them

## Strategy 1: Mirror the Job Description Language

**Bad approach:** Use your own terminology
> Resume: "Led engineering initiatives"
> Job posting: "Drive product development"

**Good approach:** Mirror their exact words
> Resume: "Drive product development initiatives"
> Job posting: "Drive product development"

**Why it works:** ATS keyword matching is literal, not semantic.

## Strategy 2: Quantify Everything

Numbers stand out and prove impact.

**Before:**
> "Improved system performance"

**After:**
> "Improved system performance by 40%, reducing latency from 2s to 1.2s for 50K daily users"

**Formula:** [Action] + [Metric] + [Scale/Context]

## Strategy 3: Reorder by Relevance, Not Chronology

If you're applying to a DevRel role but your most recent job was engineering management, **lead with your DevRel experience** even if it's older.

**Master resume order:** Chronological (newest first)
**Tailored resume order:** Most relevant first

## Strategy 4: STAR Method for Achievements

**Situation** → **Task** → **Action** → **Result**

**Example:**
> "Faced declining developer engagement (S), tasked with rebuilding workshop program (T), designed hands-on RAG demo series (A), achieving 4.8/5 satisfaction and 40% trial conversion lift (R)"

## Strategy 5: Skills Section Prioritization

Don't list skills alphabetically. List by job requirements order.

**Job requirements:**
1. Python
2. MongoDB
3. Public speaking

**Your skills section:**
```
Python, MongoDB, Public Speaking, JavaScript, AWS, Docker...
```
(Match their order exactly)

## Strategy 6: Remove Irrelevant Experience

If you have 15 years of experience but only 10 are relevant, **cut the old stuff**.

**Exception:** Keep if it shows progression/promotions, even if less relevant.

## Strategy 7: Use Their Job Title in Your Summary

**Bad:**
> "Software engineer with 10 years experience"

**Good (applying to "Senior Developer Advocate"):**
> "Senior Developer Advocate with 10 years technical experience..."

## Strategy 8: Two-Column Keyword Mapping

Create a mapping doc while tailoring:

| Job Posting Keyword | My Resume Term | Rewrite To |
|---------------------|----------------|------------|
| "vector search" | "semantic search" | "vector search" |
| "customer workshops" | "training sessions" | "customer workshops" |
| "RAG pipelines" | "retrieval systems" | "RAG pipelines" |

Then find/replace across your resume.

## Strategy 9: Cover Letter Must Reference Company Specifics

**Generic (bad):**
> "I'm excited to join your team..."

**Specific (good):**
> "I'm excited to contribute to MongoDB's recent Atlas Vector Search launch, having built similar RAG systems at..."

**How to research:**
- Company blog recent posts
- Product announcements
- LinkedIn company updates
- Recent funding/news

## Strategy 10: A/B Test Your Approach

Try different emphasis for similar roles:

**Version A:** Emphasize technical depth (code samples, architecture)
**Version B:** Emphasize developer relations (workshops, content, community)

Track response rates. Double down on what works.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Keyword Stuffing
**Bad:**
> "Expert in Python, MongoDB, vector search, RAG, semantic search, embeddings, LangChain, OpenAI, Voyage, HNSW, cosine similarity..."

**Good:**
> "Built semantic search platform using MongoDB Atlas Vector Search, integrating RAG patterns via LangChain"

### Mistake 2: Vague Achievements
**Bad:**
> "Improved developer experience"

**Good:**
> "Improved developer onboarding, reducing time-to-first-query from 2 hours to 15 minutes (4.6/5 satisfaction)"

### Mistake 3: Ignoring ATS Formatting
**Bad:**
- Resume in tables
- Graphics and logos
- Fancy fonts
- Text in headers/footers

**Good:**
- Plain text or clean Markdown
- Standard fonts
- No graphics
- All content in body

### Mistake 4: Not Proofreading
One typo can disqualify you. **Always proofread 3 times:**
1. Read for content
2. Read backwards (catches typos)
3. Read aloud (catches awkward phrasing)

## Tailoring Checklist

Before submitting:
- [ ] Job posting keywords appear naturally in resume
- [ ] All achievements quantified with numbers
- [ ] Experience reordered by relevance
- [ ] Skills prioritized by job requirements
- [ ] Summary customized to role
- [ ] Cover letter addresses hiring manager by name
- [ ] Cover letter references company-specific detail
- [ ] No typos or grammar errors
- [ ] ATS-friendly formatting (no tables/graphics)
- [ ] File named professionally (FirstLast-Resume.pdf)

## Time Investment

**Worth it:** Applying to dream job or competitive role
- Time: 2-3 hours per application
- Success rate: 30-50% interview rate

**Not worth it:** Spray-and-pray approach
- Time: 10 minutes per application
- Success rate: 2-5% interview rate

**Rule of thumb:** Quality applications > quantity applications.
