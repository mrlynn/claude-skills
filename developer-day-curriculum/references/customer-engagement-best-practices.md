# Customer Engagement Best Practices

Field-tested patterns from 50+ MongoDB developer days.

## Pre-Workshop (2 weeks before)

### Audience Research
- **Tech stack survey:** What languages/frameworks do they use?
- **Experience check:** Beginner, intermediate, advanced?
- **Goals questionnaire:** What do they want to learn?
- **Constraints:** Time, environment, restrictions

### Environment Prep
- **Setup guide:** Send 3 days before
- **Pre-check:** Test their environment 1 day before
- **Backup plan:** Local MongoDB if Atlas blocked by firewall

### Materials Ready
- Slides finalized
- Exercises tested on fresh machine
- Sample data prepared
- Backup demos ready

## Day-Of Setup (30 min before)

### Tech Check
- [ ] Projector/screen working
- [ ] Wi-Fi tested and shared
- [ ] Exercise repo accessible
- [ ] MongoDB Atlas/local running
- [ ] Backup demos loaded

### Room Setup
- Tables for laptops (not classroom style)
- Power strips accessible
- Whiteboard/markers ready
- Water/coffee available

## Opening (First 15 min)

### Introductions
- Your background (30 sec, credibility building)
- Participant introductions (name, role, goals)
- Icebreaker if >10 people

### Set Expectations
- Agenda overview
- Learning objectives
- "Ask questions anytime" culture
- Breaks schedule
- Where to find help

## During Workshop

### Energy Management
- **9-11 AM:** High energy (complex topics)
- **11-12 PM:** Energy dips (interactive exercises)
- **12-1 PM:** Lunch
- **1-2 PM:** Post-lunch slump (hands-on, not lecture)
- **2-4 PM:** Energy rebounds (advanced topics)

### Reading the Room
**Signs of confusion:**
- Furrowed brows
- Side conversations
- Blank stares
- No one attempting exercise

**Response:** Slow down, repeat, ask for questions.

**Signs of boredom:**
- Checking phones
- Laptops closed
- Side projects

**Response:** Speed up, add challenge, skip ahead.

### Handling Questions

**Good question:** Answer immediately
**Off-topic:** "Great question, let's discuss during break"
**Advanced:** "Let's cover that in the advanced section"
**Unknown:** "I don't know, let me find out and follow up"

Never fake knowledge. Admitting "I don't know" builds trust.

## Exercise Facilitation

### Before Exercise
1. Demo the exercise goal (show expected result)
2. Explain success criteria
3. Point to starter code location
4. Set timer (creates urgency)

### During Exercise
- Walk around, observe screens
- Identify common blockers
- Help individuals (quietly)
- Don't solve for them (guide with questions)

### After Exercise
- Ask who completed it (gauge difficulty)
- Review solution live
- Highlight common mistakes
- Show extensions for fast finishers

## Handling Technical Issues

### Connectivity Problems
**Backup:** Pre-downloaded MongoDB + sample data

### Dependency Failures
**Backup:** Pre-built Docker container

### Code Not Working
**Backup:** Pre-tested solution repo participants can clone

## Break Management

**Every 90 min minimum**

**Break activities:**
- Stretch
- Check emails
- Debug their own issues
- One-on-one help

## Lunch (Full-Day Workshops)

**Don't skip lunch.** Hungry participants don't learn.

**Lunch strategies:**
- Catered > everyone finding food (saves time)
- 60 minutes (not 30)
- Optional: Eat with participants (relationship building)

## Closing (Last 15 min)

### Wrap-Up
- Review what was covered
- Recap learning objectives
- Answer final questions

### Next Steps
- Share resources (slides, repo, docs)
- Suggest learning path
- Provide contact info
- Schedule optional office hours

### Feedback Collection
- Quick survey (3-5 questions)
- "One thing to improve" free text
- Contact info for follow-up

## Post-Workshop Follow-Up

### Same Day
- Email slides + exercise repo
- Thank you message
- Feedback survey link

### 24 Hours Later
- Personal thank you from you
- Additional resources
- Answers to unresolved questions

### One Week Later
- "How's it going?" check-in
- Offer help on real projects
- Share related content

### One Month Later
- Follow-up on progress
- Offer advanced workshop
- Case study opportunity

## Common Challenges

### Challenge: Mixed Skill Levels
**Solution:** Layered exercises (core + extensions)

### Challenge: Time Overrun
**Solution:** Skip less critical topics, not exercises

### Challenge: Disengaged Participants
**Solution:** Direct questions, pair programming

### Challenge: Broken Environment
**Solution:** Pair with working participant, fix during break

### Challenge: Hostile Questions
**Solution:** Acknowledge, redirect to offline discussion

## Metrics to Track

**During:**
- Exercise completion rate
- Questions per topic (gauge difficulty)
- Break timing (running late?)

**After:**
- NPS score (would recommend?)
- Learning objectives met (self-assessment)
- Most/least valuable topics

**Long-term:**
- Follow-up engagement
- Feature adoption
- Expansion opportunities

## Cultural Considerations

**Enterprise:** Formal, structured, respect hierarchy
**Startup:** Casual, fast-paced, interactive
**Academia:** Theory-focused, citation-heavy
**Government:** Compliance-aware, security-focused

Adapt tone and examples to audience culture.

## Red Flags

Watch for:
- **Legal/compliance questions:** Defer to their team
- **Competitive bashing:** Stay neutral
- **Scope creep:** "Can you also cover X?" (politely decline or schedule follow-up)

## Success Indicators

You nailed it if:
- ✅ Participants completed exercises
- ✅ Questions were engaged, not confused
- ✅ Energy stayed high
- ✅ Feedback is positive
- ✅ Follow-up requests come in
